# Examples Guide

Examples are not marketing claims. They are small, inspectable fixtures that show what Loop Creator leaves behind when AI work needs verification.

## Available examples

### `examples/ai-native-verification-tax/`

Shows the shift from prompt-only work to a verification-ready loop package.

Use it to explain:

- why Verification Tax appears after AI adoption
- how cognitive debt and intent debt show up in ordinary AI work
- what files a reviewer can inspect before trusting an AI-produced artifact
- why `runner/loop.yaml` is planning metadata, not execution proof

## Reading order

1. `before-prompt-only.md` — the fragile one-prompt version.
2. `after-loop-package/state/goal-contract.md` — what the work is supposed to accomplish.
3. `after-loop-package/eval/eval_spec.yaml` — how the result would be checked.
4. `after-loop-package/state/evidence-ledger.json` — what evidence exists and what is still only a fixture.
5. `after-loop-package/final/user-facing-summary.md` — what a human reviewer should believe now.

## Boundary

Example packages are demonstration fixtures. They are not proof that the sample artifact passed a live buyer review or external benchmark.
